# Modality Pack: Strength

## Performance Determinants

- Movement quality, force capacity, tissue tolerance, progressive loading, recovery from soreness, and exercise selection fit.

## Adaptations

- Strength maintenance can be achieved with low weekly dose when intensity and movement quality are preserved.
- Hypertrophy support needs enough volume but competes with endurance recovery when lower-body work is excessive.
- Maximal strength work has high neural and tissue cost and needs spacing from endurance quality sessions.

## Intensity Model

- Use RPE, reps in reserve, load progression, and technique quality.
- Moderate effort is often enough for support strength in hybrid plans.
- Avoid training to failure when endurance quality or consistency is the priority.

## Archetypes

- `strength_full_body_support`: broad movement quality and force exposure, 35-60 minutes, moderate fatigue.
- `strength_hypertrophy_support`: muscle-retention or definition support, 40-70 minutes, moderate-to-high fatigue.
- `strength_maintenance`: low-dose retained strength, 25-45 minutes, low-to-moderate fatigue.
- `strength_lower_body_power`: high neural cost, only when lower-body recovery budget supports it.
- `strength_mobility_preparation`: warm-up, mobility, and tissue prep, 15-30 minutes, low fatigue.

## Weekly Structure

- Two full-body sessions are often enough for mixed fitness when strength matters.
- One full-body support session can preserve continuity during cycling or running emphasis.
- Heavy lower-body work should be spaced from hard cycling or hard running.

## Common Mistakes

- Too much lower-body soreness before endurance quality.
- Chasing hypertrophy and endurance peaks simultaneously without prioritization.
- Frequent exercise novelty that prevents progression.
