# Modality Pack: Running

## Performance Determinants

- Aerobic base, tissue tolerance, running economy, threshold, VO2max, impact management, and consistency.

## Adaptations

- Easy running supports aerobic development and durability but has more impact cost than cycling.
- Strides can maintain neuromuscular quality with low volume.
- Tempo and threshold improve sustained running output.
- VO2 running is potent but carries impact and recovery risk in mixed plans.

## Intensity Model

- Use RPE, talk test, pace, heart rate, and impact response.
- Easy runs should feel conversational.
- Faster work should be introduced only when tissue tolerance is visible.

## Archetypes

- `running_easy_aerobic`: low-intensity aerobic support, 20-60 minutes, low-to-moderate fatigue.
- `running_strides`: short neuromuscular touches after easy work, low total volume, moderate neural cost.
- `running_tempo`: sustained controlled work, 30-60 minutes, moderate-to-high fatigue.
- `running_vo2_intervals`: high aerobic power, high fatigue and impact risk, rarely appropriate as optional support.
- `running_walk_run`: conservative re-entry or consistency support, 20-45 minutes, low fatigue.

## Weekly Structure

- In cycling-primary or strength-primary plans, running is often optional aerobic support unless the goal explicitly promotes it.
- Do not use running volume as casual filler when impact tolerance is uncertain.

## Common Mistakes

- Adding hard running on top of hard cycling.
- Increasing impact exposure too quickly.
- Treating running as interchangeable with cycling when tissue cost differs.
