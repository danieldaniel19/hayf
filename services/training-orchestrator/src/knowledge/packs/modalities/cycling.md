# Modality Pack: Cycling

## Performance Determinants

- Aerobic durability, sustainable power, climbing-specific power-to-weight, threshold tolerance, VO2max, and fueling tolerance.

## Adaptations

- Easy endurance supports aerobic base and repeatability.
- Tempo and threshold improve sustained output and climbing resilience.
- VO2 intervals improve high-end aerobic power and should be protected from excessive fatigue.
- Neuromuscular work can support cadence, sprint ability, and recruitment but is optional for most mixed goals.

## Intensity Model

- Use RPE, talk test, heart-rate zones, or power zones when available.
- Easy endurance should feel conversational.
- Threshold should be hard but controlled.
- VO2 should be high quality, repeatable, and not turned into all-out failure.

## Archetypes

- `cycling_endurance_ride`: easy aerobic durability, 45-150 minutes, low-to-moderate fatigue.
- `cycling_tempo_ride`: sustained aerobic pressure, 40-90 minutes, moderate fatigue.
- `cycling_threshold_intervals`: controlled hard work, 45-90 minutes, high fatigue.
- `cycling_vo2_intervals`: high-end aerobic power, 45-75 minutes, high fatigue, one exposure per week for mixed goals.
- `cycling_recovery_spin`: low-load circulation, 20-45 minutes, low fatigue.

## Weekly Structure

- For mixed goals, protect one cycling quality or endurance anchor before optional cycling volume.
- Do not stack hard cycling beside hard lower-body strength when alternatives exist.

## Common Mistakes

- Too many hard rides.
- Turning easy rides into tempo.
- Adding cycling volume while strength soreness or recovery caveats are worsening.
